What Is the MDCAT 2018 Past Paper?

The MDCAT 2018 paper was held on 23 September 2018 by the University of Health Sciences (UHS), Lahore, as the entrance test for admission to MBBS and BDS programs in Punjab's medical and dental colleges. It was open to both FSc and Non-FSc students, and this particular version is Paper Code C, complete with the university's officially released answer key.

Like all UHS entrance tests of that period, it remains one of the most downloaded MDCAT past papers because it reflects the exact examiner style, difficulty level, and recurring concept patterns that still influence the current national exam conducted under the Pakistan Medical and Dental Council (PMDC).

Unattempted questions scored zero. This makes the answer key especially useful for practicing accuracy-focused attempts rather than guessing.

Topics Covered in the MDCAT 2018 Paper

Biology

The Biology section covered heart and circulatory anatomy, enzyme kinetics and inhibition, meiosis and crossing-over, respiratory diseases like emphysema, protein structure and peptide bonds, PCR and recombinant DNA technology, immune responses (cell-mediated vs. humoral), skeletal classification, osmoregulation, hormonal control of reproduction, and ecological succession.

Example concept tested: Several questions combined structural and functional biology — for instance, identifying which enzyme deficiency causes Severe Combined Immunodeficiency Disease, or how many sodium ions are pumped out per two potassium ions in the Na⁺/K⁺ pump — the kind of applied physiology question still common in MDCAT Biology practice sets.

Chemistry

Chemistry questions ranged across reaction order and rate laws, esterification mechanisms, nucleophiles vs. electrophiles, solubility product (Ksp), the Contact Process catalyst, coordination compound geometry, polymer classification (addition vs. condensation), electronic configuration of transition metals like chromium, and calculation-based problems on moles, molarity, and gas laws.

Physics

The Physics section tested photon energy and photoelectric effect calculations, specific heat capacities (Cv and Cp), op-amp and amplifier circuits, angular motion and Coulomb's law, Lenz's law, stress-strain behavior of materials, radioactive decay and half-life, de Broglie wavelength, and electromagnetic induction — a strong mix of conceptual and numerical items.

English

The English portion emphasized correct preposition usage, verb tense and modal errors, "spot the error" sentence analysis, and vocabulary-in-context questions (e.g., words like "hiatus," "lampoon," and "encumber") — the same recurring format seen in recent MDCAT English sections.

Why This Past Paper Is Still Useful

Even though the exam is now conducted nationally by PMDC with a different question count, the MDCAT 2018 paper remains a strong practice resource because:

  • It's one of the few older papers released with a full, official answer key, letting you self-grade with confidence.
  • The FSc Pre-Medical syllabus it draws from hasn't changed dramatically, so most Biology, Chemistry, and Physics concepts are still tested today.
  • Its negative-marking format is excellent for training exam discipline — you learn to skip a question rather than guess blindly.
  • It exposes you to numerical/applied questions (photon energy, circuits, decay constants) that many students underprepare for.

Frequently Asked Questions

Who conducted the MDCAT 2018 exam?
The University of Health Sciences (UHS), Lahore conducted the MDCAT 2018 entrance test on 23 September 2018 for admissions to medical and dental colleges in Punjab.

How many questions were in the MDCAT 2018 paper, and how were they marked?
The paper had 220 MCQs worth 1100 total marks (5 marks per correct answer), with 1 mark deducted for every wrong answer and no penalty for unattempted questions.

Does this PDF include the answer key?
Yes — this download includes the official UHS answer key for Paper Code C, released alongside the exam.

Is the MDCAT 2018 pattern the same as the current MDCAT?
No. The current national MDCAT, regulated by PMDC, uses 180 MCQs across five sections and has no negative marking. Always confirm the latest pattern on the official PMDC portal before your exam.

Can I still use the MDCAT 2018 paper to prepare for the current exam?
Yes. Most of its Biology, Chemistry, and Physics questions are conceptually aligned with the current FSc Pre-Medical syllabus, making it a solid practice resource even though the exact format has since changed.
