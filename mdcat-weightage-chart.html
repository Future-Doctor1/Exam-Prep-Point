The weightage chart is the single most useful page in the entire MDCAT curriculum document — more useful, arguably, than the unit lists themselves. It tells you exactly how the 180 marks are split, which means it tells you exactly where your study hours are worth the most. This article walks through that chart number by number, plus the difficulty split that most students never factor into their prep at all.

A useful way to read this: Biology and Chemistry together account for 70% of the entire exam — 126 out of 180 marks. If your strategy is "spend time roughly proportional to marks," these two subjects alone should take up most of your study calendar.

03 — DIFFICULTY DISTRIBUTION

The 15 / 70 / 15 split

Weightage tells you where the marks are. Difficulty distribution tells you what kind of questions to expect once you're there. PM&DC fixes this ratio across the whole paper:

15% Easy
70% Moderate
15% Difficult
Easy — 15%

≈ 27 MCQs

Direct recall of definitions, structures, and stated facts. These should be near-automatic if you've read the curriculum's learning outcomes properly — they're the marks you can't afford to lose.

Moderate — 70%

≈ 126 MCQs

Applied understanding — using a concept rather than just naming it. This is the bulk of the exam, and the reason broad, even coverage beats deep expertise in a few favorite chapters.

Difficult — 15%

≈ 27 MCQs

Multi-step reasoning, often combining two concepts in one question. These differentiate top scorers — worth attempting, but not worth over-indexing your prep time on.

Strategy

Secure, then stretch

Lock in the easy 15% first on test day, work methodically through the moderate 70%, and treat the final 15% as bonus marks rather than a requirement to pass.

05 — HOW TO USE THIS CHART

Turning the numbers into a study plan

  • 01Allocate study time proportionally, not equally. Five subjects does not mean 20% effort each — Biology alone deserves close to half your revision hours.
  • 02Don't skip English and Logical Reasoning entirely. At 5% each, they're low-weight but high-efficiency — the content is narrow enough that a few focused sessions can secure most of those 18 marks.
  • 03Build accuracy on moderate-level questions first. With 70% of the paper sitting there, that's where the real score swing happens — not on the rare difficult outlier.
  • 04Treat easy marks as non-negotiable. Losing easy-tier marks to carelessness is the most avoidable way to drop your overall percentage.
  • 05Use the chart to set a weekly rhythm. A simple split like 45% Biology / 25% Chemistry / 20% Physics / 10% English & Logical Reasoning mirrors the actual exam and avoids over- or under-preparing any one subject.
