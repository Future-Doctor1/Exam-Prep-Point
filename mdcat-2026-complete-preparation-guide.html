MDCAT 2026 Exam Overview

The MDCAT (Medical & Dental College Admission Test) is the single entry test that decides admission into every public and private medical & dental college in Pakistan. It's conducted under the Pakistan Medical & Dental Council (PM&DC) pattern, and the format has stayed fairly consistent in recent years:

  • 180 MCQs across 5 subjects, single correct-answer, no negative marking
  • 3-hour duration — roughly 1 minute per MCQ on average
  • 55% pass marks required for MBBS/BDS eligibility (varies slightly by province/institution)
  • Difficulty split: 15% Easy · 70% Moderate · 15% Difficult

The syllabus is drawn from FSc Part 1 & Part 2 Biology, Chemistry, and Physics, plus dedicated English and Logical Reasoning components — but MDCAT has its own officially defined curriculum, which doesn't map 1:1 onto your FSc textbooks. Always prepare from the actual PM&DC MDCAT curriculum document, not just your college notes.

Month-by-Month Study Plan

This assumes a 4–5 month runway. Compress or stretch proportionally based on how much time you actually have — the sequence matters more than the exact dates.

Month 1–2

Concept Building

Go unit by unit through Biology, Chemistry, and Physics. Prioritize high-weightage units (Biological Molecules, Cell Structure, Chemical Bonding, Force & Motion). Take a chapter test immediately after finishing each unit — don't wait until "later."

Month 3

Consolidation

Finish remaining units. Start layering in English and Logical Reasoning practice (these need far less time but shouldn't be left to the last week). Attempt your first Quarter Test to check retention across combined subjects.

Month 4

Integration

Move to Half Book Tests and then Full Book Tests per subject. Start solving past papers year-wise — this is where you learn the examiner's actual phrasing and recurring question patterns.

Final 3–4 Weeks

Full-Length Simulation

Take Mock Tests and Full Syllabus Tests under strict 3-hour, no-pause conditions. Review every wrong answer the same day. In the last week, switch to one-page revision sheets only — no new content.

Subject-Wise Strategy

🧬 Biology

Highest ROI subject. Focus hardest on Biological Molecules, Cell Structure, Coordination & Control, Inheritance, and Immunity — these are consistently high-weightage and frequently repeated in past papers.

⚗️ Chemistry

Organic chemistry (Hydrocarbons through Carboxylic Acids) and Chemical Bonding carry the most MCQs. Don't skip numericals in Equilibrium and Electrochemistry — they show up more than students expect.

⚛️ Physics

Force & Motion, Work & Energy, Waves, and Electrostatics are the biggest units. Physics MCQs are formula-heavy — memorize derivations, not just final formulas, since MDCAT sometimes tests the "why."

📝 English

Only 9 MCQs, but they're easy marks if prepared. Build a habit of daily vocabulary practice and revise grammar rules (tenses, active/passive) rather than trying to "read more" generally.

🧠 Logical Reasoning

Pattern-recognition and critical thinking questions reward practice more than raw knowledge. Work through our Logical Reasoning Guide to learn the recurring question types.

📜 Past Papers

Once concepts are solid, past papers are non-negotiable. Start with our UHS unit-wise past papers, then move to full year-wise papers in the Past Papers section.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • ✗ Reading a unit multiple times without testing yourself — recognition ≠ recall under exam pressure
  • ✗ Ignoring English and Logical Reasoning until the last week — 18 easy MCQs left on the table
  • ✗ Jumping straight to full mocks before finishing chapter-wise practice — you'll just relearn your weak units the hard way
  • ✗ Skipping past papers entirely — MDCAT reuses concepts and phrasing more than students assume
  • ✗ Not simulating the actual 3-hour timer even once before test day — pacing is a skill, not an instinct
  • ✓ Test yourself after every unit, not just before the exam
  • ✓ Track your weak units and revisit them weekly, not just once
  • ✓ Simulate real exam conditions at least 3–4 times before test day

Frequently Asked Questions

How many months are enough to prepare for MDCAT 2026?

Most successful candidates prepare seriously for 4–6 months. Focused revision-only students have cleared it in 40–60 days if their FSc concepts were already strong — see our 40-day plan for that scenario.

Which subject has the highest weightage in MDCAT?

Biology, at 45% (81 of 180 MCQs) — nearly as much as Chemistry and Physics combined. It should get the largest share of your daily study time.

Is MDCAT 2026 based on the FSc syllabus only?

No. MDCAT follows its own officially defined PM&DC curriculum, which overlaps heavily with FSc Part 1 & 2 but isn't identical to it. Always cross-check against the official curriculum document.

Should I attempt past papers before or after finishing the syllabus?

After you've covered a unit's concepts at least once. Past papers are best used to test and reinforce what you've studied, not as your first exposure to a topic.
