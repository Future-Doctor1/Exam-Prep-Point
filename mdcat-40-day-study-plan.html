Most "40-day plans" floating around are just the chapter list cut into 40 equal pieces — which quietly punishes you, because Biology, Chemistry, and English do not carry equal weight in the actual exam. This plan does it differently: every day is sized according to the official PM&DC weightage chart, so by Day 40 your time investment mirrors the exam's real 45/25/20/5/5 split, not an arbitrary one.

04 — HOW TO RUN THIS PLAN

Rules that make the schedule actually work

Daily structure

Read → Solve → Test

Split each day into three blocks: read the unit's concepts, work through solved examples, then attempt 30–40 MCQs on that exact topic before moving on.

Flagging

Keep a running weak-list

Every day, note any topic you got under 70% accuracy on. This list is exactly what Days 37–38 are for — don't re-read everything, just your flagged gaps.

No skipped days

Protect Day 1 momentum

Missing a day early compounds — the schedule has no slack built in. If you fall behind, compress two light Chemistry or Physics days rather than dropping a Biology day.

Mock test discipline

Simulate real conditions

Days 39–40 should be done at the same time of day as your actual exam, on paper, with no breaks — the format matters as much as the content by this point.

05 — KEY TAKEAWAYS

What makes this plan different

  • 01Day-count mirrors mark-count. 16 Biology days for 45% of the exam, 10 Chemistry days for 25%, 8 Physics days for 20% — no subject is over- or under-served relative to its actual weight.
  • 02Every official unit gets a dedicated slot. All 16 Biology units, 20 Chemistry units, and 16 Physics units from the PM&DC curriculum appear by name — nothing is silently skipped.
  • 03Revision isn't an afterthought. Two full days are reserved purely for revisiting your own flagged weak points, not re-reading the whole syllabus from scratch.
  • 04Two full mock tests close the plan. Timed, 180-MCQ, paper-based simulations in the final two days build the stamina and pacing the real 3-hour exam demands.
  • 05It's adaptable, not rigid. If you start with stronger Physics or weaker Biology, shift a day or two between phases — just keep the total day-to-weight ratio roughly intact.
