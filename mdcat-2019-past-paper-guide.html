What Is the MDCAT 2019 Past Paper?

The MDCAT 2019 paper was administered by the University of Health Sciences (UHS), Lahore, for admission to MBBS and BDS programs in Punjab's public and private medical colleges. Like other MDCAT papers of that era, it consisted of 200 multiple-choice questions worth 200 marks, to be completed in 150 minutes, covering Biology, Chemistry, Physics, and English.

Even though the national entry test is now regulated by the Pakistan Medical and Dental Council (PMDC) with a slightly different paper structure, the 2019 UHS paper remains one of the most searched MDCAT past papers because its question style and difficulty level closely resemble what still appears in the current exam.

Topics Covered in the MDCAT 2019 Paper

Biology

The Biology section in this paper drew heavily from cell structure and organelles, the phases of mitosis and meiosis, DNA replication and transcription, Mendelian genetics and sex-linked inheritance, human physiology (nervous, muscular, excretory, and reproductive systems), plant physiology such as transpiration and xerophyte adaptations, immunity, and basic biotechnology concepts like PCR and recombinant plasmids.

Example concept tested: A genetics question asked students to work out the proportion of affected offspring when two heterozygous carriers for a recessive condition (such as sickle-cell trait) have children — a classic Punnett-square style problem that still appears in MDCAT Biology practice sets every year.

Chemistry

Chemistry questions spanned organic reactions (hydrolysis, esterification, nucleophilic addition), functional group identification, industrial processes like the Contact Process, reaction kinetics and rate equations, atomic structure and electronic configuration, chemical bonding (VSEPR shapes and hybridization), and calculation-based questions on molarity, moles, and isotopic abundance.

Physics

The Physics portion tested projectile motion, elastic collisions, simple harmonic motion, electromagnetic waves and their wavelength–frequency relationship, circuit and transformer principles, photon energy calculations, and error/uncertainty analysis in measurements — all core areas that remain part of the MDCAT Physics syllabus today.

English

The English section focused on vocabulary-in-context questions, correct use of punctuation and reported speech, verb tense errors, and "spot the error" items where students identify the incorrectly used segment of a sentence — a format that continues to appear in recent MDCAT papers.

Why Solving Old MDCAT Papers Still Helps

Even though the exam has since moved under PMDC's national administration with an updated 180-MCQ format, past papers like MDCAT 2019 remain valuable because:

  • The core FSc Pre-Medical syllabus (Biology, Chemistry, Physics) hasn't changed dramatically, so the same concepts keep reappearing.
  • Reviewing older papers helps you recognize recurring question styles — especially calculation-based genetics, organic chemistry conversions, and physics numericals.
  • It builds timed-practice stamina, which is essential since MDCAT is a single-sitting, negative-marking-free MCQ exam.
  • It shows you exactly how examiners phrase tricky conceptual questions, rather than straightforward textbook definitions.

How to Use This Past Paper in Your Preparation

  1. Attempt it like a real exam first. Time yourself for 150 minutes before checking any answers.
  2. Review every wrong answer conceptually — don't just memorize the correct option; understand why the distractors are wrong.
  3. Group questions by topic (e.g., all genetics questions, all organic chemistry questions) to spot your weak areas.
  4. Cross-check with the current syllabus on the official PMDC website so you focus only on topics still examinable.
  5. Combine with recent past papers from our MDCAT past papers archive for a fuller picture of question trends.

Frequently Asked Questions

Who conducted the MDCAT 2019 exam?
The University of Health Sciences (UHS), Lahore conducted the MDCAT 2019 paper for admissions to medical and dental colleges in Punjab.

How many questions were in the MDCAT 2019 paper?
The paper had 200 multiple-choice questions worth 200 marks, to be completed in 150 minutes.

Is the MDCAT 2019 pattern the same as the current MDCAT?
Not exactly. The exam is now conducted nationally by PMDC with 180 MCQs across five sections (Biology, Chemistry, Physics, English, and Logical Reasoning). Check the latest pattern on the official PMDC portal before relying solely on the 2019 pattern.

Can I still use the MDCAT 2019 paper to prepare for the current exam?
Yes. Since it draws from the same FSc Pre-Medical textbooks, most of its Biology, Chemistry, and Physics questions are still conceptually relevant for practice, even though a few older topics may have been revised out of the current syllabus.
