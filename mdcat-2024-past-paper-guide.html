What Is the MDCAT 2024 Past Paper?

The MDCAT 2024 paper was held on 22 September 2024 by the University of Health Sciences (UHS), Lahore, for admission to MBBS and BDS programs in Punjab's medical and dental colleges. This is one of the most recent, and therefore most relevant, entries in the MDCAT past papers archive — its structure and difficulty level closely mirror what students preparing for the current test conducted under the Pakistan Medical and Dental Council (PMDC) should expect.

Topics Covered in the MDCAT 2024 Paper

Biology

The Biology section covered virus structure and classification, the Calvin cycle and light-dependent reactions of photosynthesis, biomolecules (mono- and polysaccharides, phospholipids), cell organelles and their functions, nerve impulse conduction, hormonal control from the pituitary gland, genetics (Hardy-Weinberg equation, natural selection, genetic drift), respiration and gas exchange, human reproductive physiology, and the skeletal and muscular systems.

Example concept tested: Several questions required applying named principles directly — such as identifying the Hardy-Weinberg equation for calculating allele frequencies, or recognizing which chromosomal abnormality (XO) causes Turner syndrome — the kind of definition-plus-application question that appears throughout MDCAT Biology practice sets.

Chemistry

Chemistry questions spanned mole concept and mass spectrometry, ideal gas behavior and deviations, hydrogen bonding and intermolecular forces, Le Chatelier's principle and the Haber process, reaction order and the Arrhenius equation, electrochemical cells, periodic trends (atomic radius, basicity of oxides), transition metal electronic configuration, and organic chemistry topics like isomerism, alkyl halide reactivity, and benzene substitution reactions.

Physics

The Physics portion tested elastic collisions, projectile motion, circular and centripetal motion, work-energy relationships, simple harmonic motion and wave properties, the Doppler effect, Coulomb's law and electric potential, Ohm's law and resistivity, magnetic flux and Lenz's law, transformers and rectification, and modern physics topics including the photoelectric effect, de Broglie wavelength, and radioactive decay.

English

The English section tested vocabulary-in-context ("surplus," "ancestor," "spill"), correct punctuation and sentence structure, subject-verb agreement, and identifying the grammatically correct version among four similar sentences — a format consistent with earlier MDCAT English sections.

Logical Reasoning

This newer section tested cause-and-effect reasoning between paired statements, letter-pattern sequences, numeric deduction from given constraints, and syllogism-style "necessarily true" conclusion questions — skills that reward practice with structured logic puzzles rather than subject-matter memorization.
